Frequently asked questions

Does ProofReady predict a mark or band?

No — and it won't, even if asked. ProofReady gives formative feedback on what's working and what to improve next, calibrated to NESA standards. Students get a clear path forward, not a number.

Does it rewrite or write students' work for them?

No. Feedback is anchored to the student's own words and tells them what to fix and why — it never rewrites their writing or drafts it for them. The work stays theirs.

How is the feedback aligned to NESA?

Feedback is written in the voice of an experienced marker and calibrated against NESA Notes from the Marking Centre, the syllabus outcomes for the course, and the marking criteria you set for each task.

How do you know the work is the student's own?

Drafts are typed in the browser — paste and drop are blocked by default and the typing is captured as students write. What reaches ProofReady is genuinely the student's own work, which is exactly what makes the feedback, and the data behind it, worth trusting.

Which subjects and year levels does it cover?

Written-response subjects across the curriculum and Mathematics, for Years 7–12. Feedback is tuned to the student's stage and the specific course they're studying.

Is student writing kept private?

Yes. Student writing is never used to train AI models and is never shared between schools. See our Privacy Policy for the full detail.

Does it work with Canvas?

Yes. ProofReady connects to Canvas via LTI 1.3 — students launch straight from an assignment, class rosters sync automatically, and marks pass back to the Canvas gradebook.
