02
Our approach

A single design question shapes every decision we make.

“How can we get the most accurate possible feedback to mimic that of a professional, experienced teacher?”
— Central design question for ProofReady
RULE 01
We don’t grade.
No marks predicted. No bands estimated. The teacher remains the assessor.
RULE 02
We don’t rewrite.
No paragraphs returned. Feedback names what to fix and why — not how.
RULE 03
Marker voice.
Every response is calibrated against NESA Notes from the Marking Centre 2021–2024.
RULE 04
3 drafts, max.
Hard cap per task. Prevents dependency. The fourth submission goes straight to the teacher.

05
How it works

Three Claude calls. One coherent response.

Each draft fans out into three parallel passes. The slowest pass sets the wait time — not the sum of the three.

1

Holistic feedback

The load-bearing call. A subject-specific persona reads the full draft against the task verb, NESA glossary, verb-depth map, SOLO taxonomy, performance bands and common pitfalls.

→ top_priority
→ improvements[]
→ strengths[]
→ verb_check
2

Criterion-by-criterion

Runs only if the task has a rubric. Returns a verdict and a single recommendation per criterion. Skipped silently when no criteria are set, so freeform tasks still work.

→ criterion[].verdict
→ criterion[].recommend
3

Inline annotations

Returns short comments pinned to verbatim quotes from the draft. Every quote is validated server-side against the source — the model is told to mark up like a teacher’s pen.

→ annotation[].quote
→ annotation[].category
→ annotation[].comment
Why it’s fast: three calls fanned out via Promise.allSettled. Wall-clock = max(p1, p2, p3) ≈ 25–40 seconds, not the 90+ seconds a sequential chain would take. Students stay in flow; teachers don’t field “is it broken?” questions.

07
Hard guardrails

Built to support learning. Not replace it.

No marks. No band predictions. Ever.
Hard-coded in the prompt. The model refuses to estimate a mark even when explicitly asked. Assessment stays with the teacher, where it belongs.
No content rewriting.
Feedback says what to fix and why. Never how. The student writes the next sentence themselves — that’s where the learning happens.
Typed-only by default.
Paste blocked. Drop blocked. Keystroke and session telemetry stored with every draft. A teacher can prove what was typed, by whom, when.
3 drafts per task, max.
Prevents feedback dependency and caps cost. After draft 3, the next submission goes directly to the teacher — no more model feedback.

Privacy & integrity

Privacy and integrity, by design.

Sydney-hosted data

Supabase Australia region. Student drafts, feedback, marks — all stored on Australian soil. Encrypted in transit and at rest.

Never used to train models

Anthropic’s API ToS forbid training on customer data. Your students’ words don’t become someone else’s model.

School-controlled retention

Delete a class, remove a student’s work, archive a term. Standard controls. We don’t hold what your school doesn’t need us to.

Aligned with NSW obligations

Designed in step with PPIPA, the NSW Department’s AI guidance for schools, and NESA assessment integrity principles.
