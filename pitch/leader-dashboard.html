Insights for school leadership.
A descriptive, school-wide view drawn from the feedback your students already receive.
No grading. No teacher rankings. No predictions.

ProofReady already gives every student criteria-anchored feedback in the voice of an experienced HSC marker. The Insights dashboard turns those thousands of individual feedback events into a school-wide picture for Heads of Department, deputies and principals — surfacing patterns in what students are being asked to do, how they're engaging with it, and what their writing keeps showing. Every tile is descriptive. Every claim is grounded in retrievable data. None of it grades a student or ranks a teacher.

What ProofReady Insights is — and isn't

  • Descriptive, not evaluative. Shows what's happening, not who to praise or blame.
  • No marks or band predictions. Ever.
  • No teacher league tables. Class-level views describe student work, not teacher quality.
  • No student rankings. Cohort-level patterns, not individual scoreboards.
  • One source of evidence among many. Supports professional judgement; never replaces it.
  • Role-scoped access. Leaders see their own school only; teachers retain their existing view.

The Student Engagement View

How students are working — without grading them. Patterns of timing, iteration, and follow-through.

Submission timing

How early or late students submit relative to due dates. Cohort and class-level patterns.

Draft progression

How many students reach a second or third draft, and which tasks prompt the most iteration.

Resubmission patterns

Rate and frequency of resubmission across the cohort — an indicator of feedback uptake.

Time-to-resubmission

How quickly students return to their work after feedback. A signal of momentum and motivation.

Feedback engagement rate

Whether students view their feedback before submitting a next draft.

Engagement consistency

Students using ProofReady regularly vs sporadically — cohort patterns, not individual surveillance.

The Feedback Theme View

The pattern layer underneath the numbers. ProofReady reads across every piece of feedback the AI has produced and surfaces what it keeps saying — per class, per cohort, per faculty.

Recurring class-level themes

The five or ten issues the AI keeps flagging in this class's drafts. Anchored to specific student work, so the patterns are verifiable.

Theme evolution over the term

What's resolving, what's persisting, what's newly emerging. Week-3 themes vs Week-10 themes, side by side.

Verb-specific pitfall patterns

When the task asks students to evaluate, what do they actually do instead? Cohort-wide misalignment, surfaced.

Subject-terminology adherence

Are students using the technical vocabulary the syllabus expects? Drawn from ProofReady's subject glossaries.

NESA key term miscue patterns

Common substitutions: students treating assess as describe, analyse as list. Specific and actionable.

Cross-class theme distinctiveness

What's unusual about this class compared to others in the faculty? Surfaces differentiation needs without ranking.

Cross-faculty literacy patterns

The same writing weakness appearing across History, English, Biology — the evidence base for whole-school literacy initiatives.

Theme × outcome correlation

Which feedback themes attach to which syllabus outcomes — useful when planning targeted teaching.

The AI Narrative Layer

The distinctive feature. Most education analytics tools produce numbers; ProofReady produces prose — short, grounded narratives written for time-poor school leaders. Every claim is anchored in the underlying data, so the reader can verify any sentence by drilling down.

Example — Term summary narrative, Year 11 Modern History Across 28 student responses to the Term 1 source analysis task, the cohort consistently identifies sources well but struggles to weigh sources against each other. When the task verb is assess, responses are dominated by description and explanation; explicit judgement language appears in fewer than a third of drafts. The pattern is consistent across all three Year 11 classes, suggesting it's a programme-level rather than class-level concern. Worth a faculty conversation about explicit modelling of evaluative phrases before the Week 7 source-based task.

Access, Scope & Privacy

Who can see what

Insights access is granted to a defined school leadership group — not all staff. Teachers retain their existing class view. Students never see leader-level data.

School-scoped

Every leader sees their own school's data only. No cross-school comparisons. No external benchmarking against schools you don't recognise.

Faculty-respectful

Class-level views describe the student work, not the teacher's performance. Comparisons are framed as patterns to explore, not judgements to act on.

No AI training on student work

Anthropic's API policy excludes API inputs and outputs from model training. Insights aggregates existing feedback — no additional data leaves the system.

Auditable

Every aggregate tile and every AI narrative claim is traceable to the underlying tasks and feedback events that produced it.

Opt-in by tile

The school chooses which Insights tiles are enabled. Start with assessment programme and themes; add others as the leadership team finds use for them.

What this isn't

No mark predictions No band judgements No teacher rankings No student league tables No cross-school benchmarking No demographic profiling without policy No content rewriting
