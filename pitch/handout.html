AI-powered formative feedback, aligned to the NESA syllabus.
Built for NSW HSC students.
Designed to mimic the feedback an experienced marker would give, before final submission.

Students rarely get individual, criteria-based feedback on a draft before they submit it for marking. Teachers don't always have time to read 30 drafts in the week before a deadline. ProofReady fills that gap: every student gets specific, written feedback on their draft within a minute, in the voice of an experienced HSC marker.

For students

  • Paste a draft, get back strengths, areas to improve, and a key-term check (e.g. analyse vs. describe)
  • An annotated copy of their own draft with margin comments, like a teacher's pen marks
  • Up to 3 drafts per task, so they see their work improve
  • No marks, no grades; only feedback to act on

For teachers

  • Create a class, share a 6-character code, students join in seconds
  • See every student's drafts and the feedback they received
  • One-click class-level synthesis: common gaps and strengths across the cohort
  • CSV export for record-keeping

For your faculty

  • Reduces draft-marking workload without removing teacher judgement
  • Surfaces common cohort gaps you can address in one lesson
  • Subject-specific calibration: English Advanced feedback differs from Biology
  • Australian context, NESA verbs, syllabus-aligned

What makes the feedback usable

ProofReady runs three independent passes on every draft: a holistic NESA-marker-voice review, a criterion-by-criterion check against the marking criteria you provide, and inline annotations attached to specific quotes from the student's writing. Subject-specific calibration draws on a curated bank of NESA Notes from the Marking Centre (2021–2024) and syllabus-defined glossaries, so an English Advanced response is engaged on textual integrity and persona, while a Biology response is held to scientific reasoning, validity, and evidence.

No mark predictions No band judgements No content rewriting Always a draft, never final
