HSC PDHPE — Core 1: Health Priorities in Australia
Analyse how the Ottawa Charter can be used to address cardiovascular disease (CVD) as a health priority in Australia. (8 marks)
Task verb: analyse
✓ What you've done well — keep these
  • You've correctly identified all five Ottawa Charter action areas and attempted to apply each one to CVD — this shows you understand the basic framework
  • Your writing is clear and easy to follow, with good paragraph structure that addresses each action area systematically
  • You've made some relevant connections, like linking healthy eating education to developing personal skills and mentioning workplace interventions for supportive environments
📝 Task verb check — "analyse"

The question asks you to analyse, which means you need to identify components and relationships between the Ottawa Charter and CVD, then draw out implications about how effective these strategies could be.

Looking at your response, you've mostly described what each action area could do rather than analysed it. For example, when you write "Developing personal skills means educating people about how to live healthier. For CVD this could include teaching people about healthy eating and exercise" — you're describing what it is and giving an example, but you're not exploring how this education actually reduces CVD risk, what the relationships are between knowledge and behaviour change, or what the implications might be for different population groups.

You need to dig deeper into the "how" and "why" rather than just the "what".

! Top priority — focus on this first

Transform your descriptive statements into analytical ones by exploring relationships and implications. For each action area, don't just say what it could do — analyse how it works, why it's effective for CVD specifically, and what this means for different population groups.

⚡ Things to improve
  • 1 Your opening paragraph lacks the epidemiological data needed for full marks. Add specific Australian statistics about CVD — mortality rates, prevalence, economic burden, and which groups are most affected (age, gender, socioeconomic status, Indigenous Australians). This demonstrates why it's a health priority beyond just saying "many people die from it".
  • 2 You need to analyse relationships and implications, not just describe. For developing personal skills, explore HOW health education leads to behaviour change, what barriers exist (health literacy, socioeconomic factors), and why this approach works better for some groups than others.
  • 3 Your supportive environments section needs deeper analysis. Don't just list examples like "parks and bike paths" — analyse how environmental changes remove barriers to healthy choices and examine which environments have the biggest impact on CVD risk.
  • 4 The community action paragraph is too generic. Analyse how community-led initiatives create social support networks, address local CVD risk factors, and reach populations that formal health services might miss. What makes community action effective for CVD specifically?
  • 5 Your reorienting health services section misses key analysis. Explore the shift from acute care to prevention, how this changes resource allocation, and analyse the effectiveness of screening programs versus treatment approaches for different CVD risk factors.
  • 6 The healthy public policy section needs specific Australian examples and deeper analysis. Mention actual policies like plain packaging laws, sugar taxes being considered, or food labelling requirements. Analyse how policy creates population-wide change versus individual approaches.
  • 7 You haven't addressed which groups are most at risk for CVD or how Ottawa Charter strategies might work differently for different populations (Indigenous Australians, low socioeconomic groups, older adults).
  • 8 Missing analysis of how the five action areas work together synergistically — this interconnectedness is crucial for full analysis.
  • 9 Your conclusion is too brief and doesn't synthesise your analysis. It should draw together the key relationships and implications you've identified.
  • 10 No specific Australian examples, programs, or current data throughout your response — this is essential for the communication criterion.
💬 Overall

This response demonstrates basic understanding of the Ottawa Charter framework but sits in the Band 3–4 range because it describes rather than analyses. You've covered the content but haven't shown the deeper thinking that analysis requires.

🎯 What a strong response to this question includes
  • Current Australian CVD statistics showing mortality, morbidity, economic burden, and which groups are most affected
  • Analysis of how each Ottawa Charter action area specifically targets CVD risk factors and the mechanisms by which they create change
  • Examination of how strategies work differently for high-risk populations (Indigenous Australians, low SES groups) and why
  • Specific Australian examples of policies, programs, or initiatives that demonstrate each action area in practice
  • Analysis of the interconnected nature of the five action areas and how they reinforce each other to address CVD comprehensively
